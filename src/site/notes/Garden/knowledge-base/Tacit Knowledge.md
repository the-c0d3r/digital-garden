# [[Garden/knowledge-base/Tacit Knowledge\|Tacit Knowledge]]

topics: [[03-Resource/knowledge/Knowledge\|Knowledge]]

source: [The Tacit Knowledge Series - Commonplace - The Commoncog Blog](https://commoncog.com/blog/the-tacit-knowledge-series/)

Tacit knowledge is knowledge that **cannot be captured through words alone**.
This is what we call it expert intuition. Based on the source blog, it seems like this can be taught, or learned.


source: [Why Tacit Knowledge is More Important Than Deliberate Practice - Commonplace - The Commoncog Blog](https://commoncog.com/blog/tacit-knowledge-is-a-real-thing/)

But not just that, it looks like it's something that we repeatedly do, in order to acquire the principles behind this action.

This is the reason why I sometimes say I cannot give a reason, but I know it is the right thing to do. Or why sometimes I know something is just weird, or not right.
